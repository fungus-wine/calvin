title: RIP Arduino

Calvin's Arduino GIGA R1 Wifi died. Last night, I burned my finger when I briefly touched the memory chip on the board. It was really, really hot. As best I can tell, that means the board is fried, even though it's still working (for now). How this happened is a mystery. I could have shorted something accidentally, miswired it, or it might just be defective. In any case, it's toast.

This gives me an opportunity to change gears in the design. I was beginning to regret going with the GIGA for a couple reasons. One was that it's expensive (~$70), and it has way more capability than I need. I primarily got it so I could use the GIGA Display Shield, and i liked the dual core architecture. But as I've gotten more into the project, the Display is less compelling. It works, but it's slow and doesn't provide the experience I thought it would. I've also moved on to a much more robust remote monitoring system (Calvin Explorator) that makes the display a whole lot less useful.

So I'm ditching the Arduino and it's display and switching over to a Teensy 4.1. It's cheaper, smaller, plenty capable, and has all the bells and whistles I need.

One downside is that I'll need to add Teensy support to `grot`, and I'm a little worried that `teensy_loader_cli` won't work on OSX 26 (`homebrew` says it will, but you never know - the official docs are very old). But one way or another I'll get it working.

The other downside is the Teensy is a single core architecture. I liked having basic/safety functions on it's own core away from less stable systems. But without the display, the M7 core was basically just a pass through to the Jetson. Two cores don't really serve a useful purpose any more. There are also some techniques to minimize any non-critical code's impact on the critical loop.

This means I have to re-write the whole event queue system. It's not a huge issue - just more work to be done.
